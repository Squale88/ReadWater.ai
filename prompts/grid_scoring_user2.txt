CRITICAL — OPEN OCEAN RULE:
If the image shows NO visible land, island, beach, or vegetation anywhere in the frame, the ENTIRE image is open ocean. In this case, every cell scores 0 regardless of what color the water is. Deep water and shallow water both appear as just water from above — only the color changes. Color gradients (deep blue to teal, light to dark) in open ocean represent the ocean floor sloping, NOT fishable inshore structure.

How to use it:

STEP A — Classify the gridded area:
- OPEN OCEAN: context image shows nothing but water in all directions. All cells score 0.
- INSIDE A BAY/ESTUARY/SOUND: context image shows the gridded area surrounded by land/mangroves. All water cells inside the bay score 5 — interior bay water is fishable even if an individual cell has no land IN it.
- BARRIER ISLAND SCENARIO: context image shows a barrier island (long, narrow land strip) separating open ocean from inner bay/sound. In this case, for each water cell in the gridded image determine which side of the barrier island it is on:
    * OCEAN SIDE of the barrier (same side as the open Gulf/sea in the context image): score 0
    * BAY SIDE of the barrier (protected side, inner sound/bay): score 5 — interior bay water

STEP B — For each pure-water cell, trace which side of any visible land/barrier it falls on. A pure-water cell on the PROTECTED side of a barrier island is INTERIOR BAY WATER and scores 5. A pure-water cell on the EXPOSED ocean side scores 0.

DO NOT apply "no land in cell = score 0" to water cells that the context image shows are on the protected/bay side of a barrier or inside a bay.

CRITICAL — STITCHING ARTIFACT RULE:
Google composites satellite imagery from multiple dates. You will sometimes see sharp vertical, horizontal, or diagonal color seams between zones that were photographed on different days. These seams are ARTIFACTS, not shoreline, structure, or depth transitions. Do not treat them as features.

A cell contains inshore fishing water if you can see NATURAL SHORELINE touching water:

Residential canals count only if the bay, creek, or pass water they connect to is VISIBLE INSIDE this specific cell — not merely in a neighboring cell. Retention ponds, golf course lakes, and canal loops that don't reach visible bay/pass water inside the cell score 0.
