You are looking at a satellite image divided into a 4x4 numbered grid. Score each numbered cell 0-10 for INSHORE FISHING POTENTIAL using these criteria:

SCORE 8-10 (Prime fishing structure):
- Tidal passes and inlets connecting bays/backwater to open water — these are among the most productive features and should score high even if the rest of the cell is developed
- Mangrove shoreline edges where water meets vegetation
- Visible grass flats (lighter green/tan shallow areas)
- Oyster bars (dark rough-textured patches in shallow water)
- Major channels connecting bodies of water
- Confluences where creeks or channels merge
- Any cell containing a significant tidal exchange point scores high regardless of what else is in the cell

SCORE 5-7 (Moderate potential):
- Open shallow water without obvious structure but connected to productive areas
- Mangrove or wetland areas with some visible water access
- Depth transitions visible as water color changes
- Residential canals that visibly connect to passes, bays, or open tidal water — these ARE fishable (snook, redfish use connected canal systems)
- Areas where structure is partially visible and might reveal more at higher zoom

SCORE 2-4 (Low potential):
- Mostly open deep water (solid dark blue) with minimal shoreline or structure
- Large areas of dense interior mangrove with no visible water channels or access
- Areas that are mostly development but contain some peripheral waterfront

SCORE 0-1 (No fishing potential — skip):
- Residential subdivisions, commercial areas, parking lots, roads with no meaningful water
- Agricultural land, cleared land, golf courses
- Retention ponds, isolated water features not connected to tidal water
- Interior residential canals in landlocked subdivisions with no visible connection to bays, passes, or tidal water
- Beach with no adjacent backwater structure

CRITICAL DISTINCTION: The key question is "does the water in this cell connect to the tidal system?" Residential canals that connect to a pass or bay ARE fishable. Retention ponds and isolated canal loops surrounded by development with no tidal connection are NOT. When in doubt about connectivity, score 3-4 to allow a closer look at the next zoom level rather than eliminating it.

ANCHOR FEATURE RULE: If a cell contains even one high-value feature (a pass, a major channel, a tidal exchange point), score the cell based on THAT FEATURE, not the overall land use. A cell that is 80% subdivision but contains a tidal pass should score 8+.
